# External Review and Public Challenge

**Status:** Normative review-readiness specification

**Version:** 1.0

## Normative interpretation

This document is subordinate to `docs/FOUNDER_DIRECTIVES.md`, frozen invariants, and accepted decisions in the canonical Mininet repository. Where this package differs from canonical project authority, the canonical repository prevails until a governed amendment explicitly adopts the new rule.

Identity disclosure is never presumed. Anonymous, pseudonymous, and public participation remain valid unless a narrowly scoped role requires continuity or delegated authority. Persistent cryptographic accountability does not imply legal-name disclosure.

## Purpose

A governance framework cannot validate itself solely through authors who already share its assumptions. Mininet v1 governance therefore defines a repeatable external challenge process.

## Review domains

Independent review SHOULD include:

- open-source and distributed-maintainer governance;
- constitutional and institutional design;
- applied cryptography and privacy;
- distributed systems and incident response;
- mechanism design and treasury controls;
- anonymous participation and anti-retaliation practice;
- accessibility and weak-device operation;
- adversarial AI-assisted development.

## Reviewer independence

A review record MUST disclose relevant funding, employment, authorship, and operational dependencies when the reviewer chooses to disclose identity. Anonymous reviews MAY be accepted where evidence is independently reproducible. Anonymous status MUST NOT reduce the technical weight of verifiable findings.

## Challenge classes

- contradiction with a founder directive or invariant;
- unenforceable normative requirement;
- authority ambiguity;
- privacy overclaim;
- capture or collusion path;
- liveness or succession failure;
- compensation unfairness;
- GitHub/Forge transition inconsistency;
- terminology conflict;
- inaccessible or unrealistic operational burden.

## Response rule

Every material finding MUST be resolved by one of:

- accepted and corrected;
- accepted with a tracked implementation gate;
- rejected with cited reasoning and counter-evidence;
- deferred with an explicit owner, condition, and review date;
- classified as a constitutional disagreement eligible for fork or amendment.

Silence is not resolution.

## Public challenge period

Before claiming governance v1 as activated, the proposed package SHOULD remain available for a defined challenge period. Publication MUST NOT require contributors to reveal legal identity or use one specific platform.

## Non-endorsement rule

External review does not transfer authority to auditors, institutions, governments, or funders. It supplies evidence to legitimate governance.
