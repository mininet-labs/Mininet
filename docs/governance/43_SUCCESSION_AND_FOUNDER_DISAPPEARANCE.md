# Succession and Founder Disappearance

**Status:** Normative continuity specification

**Version:** 1.0

## Normative interpretation

This document is subordinate to `docs/FOUNDER_DIRECTIVES.md`, frozen invariants, and accepted decisions in the canonical Mininet repository. Where this package differs from canonical project authority, the canonical repository prevails until a governed amendment explicitly adopts the new rule.

Identity disclosure is never presumed. Anonymous, pseudonymous, and public participation remain valid unless a narrowly scoped role requires continuity or delegated authority. Persistent cryptographic accountability does not imply legal-name disclosure.

## Principle

Founder authority is bootstrap defense, not inheritable ownership. The disappearance, incapacity, compromise, or refusal of the Founder MUST NOT permanently halt legitimate development.

## Preparation requirements

Before Founder authority is reduced, the project SHOULD maintain:

- at least two independent recovery custodians for operational access;
- documented repository and domain recovery procedures;
- independent copies of canonical history and governance evidence;
- delegated working-group authorities with expirations;
- release authority separate from a single personal account;
- treasury controls that do not transfer through personal inheritance;
- a public succession-state record.

## Disappearance trigger

Disappearance MUST NOT be inferred from short inactivity. A transition rule SHOULD combine:

- missed signed liveness statements or governance duties;
- direct recovery attempts;
- defined waiting period;
- independent attestations;
- absence of conflicting authenticated activity.

## Transition order

1. Suspend founder-only operations that are unsafe without confirmation.
2. Preserve all data and prevent irreversible account transfer.
3. Activate previously delegated, limited recovery authority.
4. Publish evidence and the exact scope of temporary authority.
5. Continue ordinary work where existing delegated rules permit it.
6. Convene the constitutional succession process.
7. Retire temporary authority after canonical governance is restored.

## No hereditary control

Legal heirs, employers, infrastructure providers, or holders of a personal account MUST NOT automatically inherit constitutional authority. They may transfer assets or credentials only according to previously defined recovery rules.

## Founder return

A returning Founder does not automatically invalidate legitimate decisions made under the succession process. Conflicts MUST be resolved through canonical governance and evidence, not personal assertion.
