# Right to Fork, Exit, and Restart

**Status:** Normative

**Version:** 1.0

## Normative interpretation

This document is subordinate to `docs/FOUNDER_DIRECTIVES.md`, frozen invariants, and accepted decisions in the canonical Mininet repository. Where this package differs from canonical project authority, the canonical repository prevails until a governed amendment explicitly adopts the new rule.

Identity disclosure is never presumed. Anonymous, pseudonymous, and public participation remain valid unless a narrowly scoped role requires continuity or delegated authority. Persistent cryptographic accountability does not imply legal-name disclosure.

## Rights

Every participant retains the right to:

- stop participating;
- stop following a community or moderation policy;
- export locally controlled data and keys where technically possible;
- reject software and governance outcomes;
- continue from an earlier lawful state;
- fork code, specifications, and governance history under the project license;
- create a new pseudonym or identity root without seeking permission.

## Limits of continuity

A new identity does not automatically inherit:

- authority;
- reputation;
- bounty claims;
- governance votes;
- treasury rights;
- another identity's accountability record.

A participant MAY prove selected continuity through voluntary cryptographic linkage or zero-knowledge evidence where available. The protocol MUST NOT force such linkage.

## Fork legitimacy

Fork legitimacy is earned through continuity, evidence, users, and governance—not through trademarks, domains, GitHub ownership, wealth, or possession of old infrastructure.

The canonical network MAY refuse to recognize a fork as its continuation. It MUST NOT prevent the fork from existing or falsely claim technical impossibility where only social disagreement exists.

## Data sovereignty

Exit MUST NOT require deletion of another participant's independently held records. Sovereignty means control over one's own data and disclosures, not unilateral rewriting of other participants' history.

## Safety and filtering choice

Users MAY subscribe to moderation, warning, reputation, age, community, or safety layers. Such layers MUST be distinguishable from base protocol validity and MUST be replaceable or rejectable whenever doing so does not violate protocol integrity.
