# Post-v1 Evolution and Open Research

**Status:** Informative with normative honesty requirements

**Version:** 1.0

## Normative interpretation

This document is subordinate to `docs/FOUNDER_DIRECTIVES.md`, frozen invariants, and accepted decisions in the canonical Mininet repository. Where this package differs from canonical project authority, the canonical repository prevails until a governed amendment explicitly adopts the new rule.

Identity disclosure is never presumed. Anonymous, pseudonymous, and public participation remain valid unless a narrowly scoped role requires continuity or delegated authority. Persistent cryptographic accountability does not imply legal-name disclosure.

## Purpose

Version 1.0 closes the constitutional framework, not the research agenda.

## Open research

- privacy-preserving human uniqueness and liveness;
- coercion-resistant delegation and voting;
- anonymous reputation without cross-context surveillance;
- private bounty settlement with public treasury accountability;
- scalable deliberation under AI-generated proposal volume;
- governance spam resistance without identity centralization;
- capture measurement across employers, funding, infrastructure, and social graphs;
- constitutional continuity across long network partitions;
- safe autonomous agents that earn compensation without gaining political authority;
- accessible governance for weak devices and low-bandwidth participants.

## Future-version rule

Future versions MAY add mechanisms, but MUST NOT retroactively claim that v1 solved open research. New evidence SHOULD narrow uncertainty rather than erase historical warnings.

## Versioning

- patch releases correct unambiguous errors without changing meaning;
- minor releases add compatible operational guidance or schemas;
- major releases change constitutional or state-machine semantics and require the amendment protocol.

## Living system

A mature governance system is expected to discover failures. The ability to record, reason about, and correct them is a strength; pretending the system is complete is a failure.
